Windows XP Baremetal Setup

As a Christmas present for my brother in 2024, I decided to get him a complete Windows XP setup, all using time-accurate hardware from the early 2000s. The acquisition of the hardware was actually rather easy, as one of my roommates mother had acquired their old office PCs, and was looking to get rid of them anyway. I took them in, and was able to salavage and test all of the parts, to frankenstein together a working system. For the peripherals, I was able to acquire the old CRT monitor, along with a keyboard, mouse, and external speakers from my grandparents, who had stopped using their desktop PC years ago.

This project helped me come to a greater understanding of older hardware and software, and their limitations, such as the 4GB single-file size limit of FAT32. It also helped me troubleshoot and identify faulty hardware components, as many of the computers did not work out of the box, as they had deteriorated while in storage, such as with leaky capacitors on the motherboard.
